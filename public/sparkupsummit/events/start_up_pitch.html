Start-Up Pitch

Description

In this event, startups present their business models to students, helping them understand entrepreneurship and team building. Participants will pitch their ideas to potential investors, fostering growth and innovation.

Rules of Procedure

  • Team Composition: Teams should consist of 3-5 members
  • Registration Fee: A registration fee of INR— applies to each participant.
  • Preliminary Submission: Teams are required to submit their presentation and any supplementary materials (such as prototypes) at least 24 hours before the event to ensure the basic eligibility criteria, such as originality and relevance.
  • Event Schedule: The event will begin with organizers explaining the flow of events, rules, and evaluation criteria, followed by the introduction of the judging panel—consisting of entrepreneurs, investors, or professors.
  • Pitching Structure: Each team is given 7 minutes to present their start-up idea using PowerPoint.During the pitch, teams present their problem statement, product/service solution, business model, target market, marketing strategy, financial projections, etc.
  • Q&A Session: After each pitch, judges are given 3 minutes to ask questions related to the start-up, business viability, or any clarifications needed.

Guidelines

  • Content Development: Your start-up idea must be original. Pre-existing ideas are accepted only if they have not been commercially launched.
  • Presentation Preparation: Teams must use clear and concise slides to ensure clarity, timing and smooth delivery. It is suggested to bring a backup copy of your presentation on a USB drive and email it to yourself as a precaution.
  • Time Management: Teams should stick to the time limit during the presentation, keeping themselves focused and prepared for the Q&A session.
  • Professional Conduct: It is advised to maintain professionalism throughout the event and show respect towards other teams, judges, and organizers.
