Bidding War

Description

Participants represent different countries in an auction style event where they bid on products from around the world. The team with the highest net worth at the end of the auction will be declared the winner.

Rules of Procedure

  • The event begins with the assignment of countries to different teams, with each team taking their position according to the designated country.
  • Based on prior discussions, the OC will release the auction board, listing the products from each respective country along with their price ranges. No changes to the prices or points will be permitted once the event begins.
  • Participating teams will bid on the products following the standard auction process to earn points.
  • Each team will be allocated an equal amount in their treasury, which they can utilise according to their strategy and interests. Each product will have a fixed price, and any team interested in purchasing it must spend the corresponding amount from its Treasury. If a team attempts to bid without sufficient funds, it will be disqualified from the event.
  • Each product will carry specific points, which will contribute to determining the winning team. The final score will be calculated by summing the remaining amount in the Treasury and the points earned through the auction.
  • The entire event will be monitored by the jury, led by the auction coordinator.

Guidelines

  • Bidding Wars is a team-based game where each team may consist of up to 4 participants.
  • The registration fee for Bidding Wars is INR per participant.
  • No changes to the prices or points will be permitted once the event begins.
  • No discussions among teams will be allowed during the event, and no outsiders will be permitted in the bidding room during the auction.
  • The total number of participating teams will determine the number of rounds or levels, which will be announced before the event begins.
  • If a team attempts to bid without sufficient funds, it will be disqualified from the event.
  • Bidding Wars simulates an auction-friendly environment. Heated debates and arguments among participants or with jury members will not be tolerated.
