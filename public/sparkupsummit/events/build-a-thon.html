Build A Thon

Description

The Build-a-Thon is an intense 12-hour overnight event where participants are given a domain out of which they will select a real-world problem statement and are tasked with developing innovative solutions. Using tools like the Business Model Canvas, Value Proposition Canvas, and prototype creation (if necessary), teams will work to design and refine their ideas. The event culminates in a pitching round, where teams present their solutions using a provided template. This fast-paced simulation immerses students in an entrepreneurial experience, challenging them to think critically and act quickly to solve real-life problems, all within a competitive and high-energy environment.

Rules of Procedure

  • Team Composition: Teams must consist of a maximum of five members.
  • Registration Fee: A registration fee of INR 200 applies to each participant.The registration will be based on first come first served basis.
  • Event Structure: a. At the start of the event, teams will receive a domain out of which they will select a real-world problem statement to work on.
    b. A briefing session will be held beforehand to explain the procedures and technicalities. Participants will receive key guidance on evaluation criteria to help them prepare effectively.
  • Pitching Session: a. After 12 hours of brainstorming and development, teams will pitch their ideas through presentations in the provided templates.
  • Timely Challenges: Throughout the event, teams will encounter timely challenges, which may include: i. Business Model Canvas ii. Value Proposition Canvas iii. Revenue Model etc.
  • Eligibility: Participation is open to teams from all colleges across the country.

Guidelines

  • Content Development: a. Solutions must be both creative and practical, addressing real-world challenges.
    b. Ideas should be scalable and sustainable, with a focus on long-term viability and impact.
  • Collaboration: Effective teamwork is essential. Regular check-ins are recommended to ensure alignment and progress.
  • Time Management: Utilize the 12-hour development period wisely to ensure thorough preparation for the pitching session.
  • Resource Utilization: Make the most of available resources, including mentorship, workshops, and materials provided. Seek guidance from mentors to refine ideas and approaches.
  • Presentation Preparation: a. Prepare clear, concise presentations that effectively communicate your ideas.
    b. Use visual aids and prototypes to enhance understanding and engagement.
  • Professional Conduct: a. Adhere to the event's code of conduct, treating judges and fellow participants with respect.
    b. Engage in ethical practices and avoid plagiarism or dishonesty.
