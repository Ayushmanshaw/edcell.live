Build A Thon

Description

Build-a-thon is a 12-hour overnight event where students will receive real-life problem statements on the spot and must develop solutions using the Business Model Canvas, create prototypes if needed, and prepare presentations to share and pitch their presentations during the pitching round.

Rules of Procedure

  • Team Composition: Teams must consist of a maximum of five members.
  • Registration Fee: A registration fee of INR 200 applies to each participant.The registration will be based on first come first serve basis.
  • Event Structure: a. At the start of the event, teams will receive a problem statement to work on.
    b. A briefing session will be held beforehand to explain the procedures and technicalities. Participants will receive key guidance on evaluation criteria to help them prepare effectively.
  • Pitching Session: a. After 12 hours of brainstorming and development, teams will pitch their startup ideas through presentations.
    b. Teams will discuss their concepts with judges and peers during this session.
  • Timely Challenges: Throughout the event, teams will encounter timely challenges, which may include: i. Business Model Canvas ii. Value Proposition Canvas iii. Revenue Model etc.
  • Eligibility: Participation is open to teams from all colleges across the country.

Guidelines

  • Content Development: a. Solutions must demonstrate creativity and practicality in addressing real-world challenges.
    b. Ideas should be scalable and sustainable, considering long-term viability and impact.
  • Collaboration: a. Teams are encouraged to collaborate effectively and utilize the resources available during the event.
    b. Regular check-ins among team members are recommended to ensure alignment and progress.
  • Time Management: Teams should manage their time wisely during the 12-hour development period to ensure thorough preparation for the pitching session.
  • Resource Utilization: a. Teams should make the most of available resources, including mentorship, workshops, and materials provided during the event.
    b. It is encouraged to seek guidance from mentors if available to refine ideas and approaches.
  • Presentation Preparation: a. Teams should prepare clear and concise presentations that effectively communicate their ideas and solutions.
    b. Visual aids and prototypes should be used to enhance understanding and engagement during pitches.
